Demo: Tunnel of Asplen (A CSCI 202 Project)

Abstract

Welcome to the Tunnel of Asplen.
The Tunnel is a more akin to a massive dungeon construct stretching below the surface of the recently discovered super Earth-like planet, Rikshasa, for an estimated 75,000 kilometers. To date, only 1% of the dungeon has been mapped with the safest depths mapped up to 400 kilometers.
You are a contracted combat patroller who has been sent to perform a routine check on the first 10 kilometers. While most of the "safe" 400 kilometers are relatively harmless, routine patrols are required to keep the advertised safety rating up.

How to Play: Basics

The game takes place in a text terminal as defined by a form submission section and an informative textarea. The terminal recognizes a set of basic commands to facilitate the simple gameplay loop. When a correct and accepted command is inputted, the main terminal will update with new information. (At the time of writing this page at 02:19 on December 1, 2022, screenreader compatibility has not been determined).

  • Move
    • This command prompts your patrol to move into the next kilometer.
    • This command must be communicated alone. It does not take any extra arguments.
    • This command cannot be used when in combat or when a Kilometer Event is first encountered.
  • Choose [choice number]
    • This command accepts ONE argument.
    • This command is specific to a Kilometer Event that provides options.
    • To select an option during a Kilometer Event, use choose [choice number]
      Replace the [choice number] field with the appropriate option digit. Do not include the brackets.
  • Progress (not implemented)
    • This command can be used at any time to generate a report.
    • This report provides the player's current health points, Kilometer Position, current Kilometer Event and Card Deck.
    • This command does not take any other arguments.
The player is allowed to choose from a set of fixed "ability cards". These ability cards are utilized during a Combat-type Kilometer Event. The Ability Cards are divided into three Categories of Support, Defend and Attack cards. A set of these cards are referred to as "Ability Card Decks" (ACD). Each card has a unique ID code that can be used to utilize its power or gain additional information.
  • Attacker
    • Has: 0,2,3,4,6
  • Defender
    • Has: 0,1,5,7,11
  • Balanced
    • Has: 0,1,3,4,5
To find more details about these card IDs, we have a compendium of all available cards (not complete).

Combat

As a patroller, you will no doubt encounter hostiles along the way. Thanks to the Combat Unit Network Technology System (C.U. Net), you can make combat decisions as if you are taking turns in a card game. In the upper depths like the ones you are patrolling, hostiles are sluggish enough that you will almost always take the first action. Your terminal will decide on potential actions based on your Ability Card Deck (ACD). These are not necessarily the best options every time as the system is still in active development. You will usually receive up to 3 card options per turn.
To make use of the cards, your terminal will accept the new command: use [card ID]
Your selected card ID can only be one from the chosen set of cards by the C.U. Net.
The enemy will follow your action with their own.
During battle, you will need to maintain your Health Point Wellness (HP). Should your HP reach zero, your command interface will disconnect and you will be dismissed from the operation. Hostiles also have HP and will expire after their perceived HP reaches zero.
To maintain your HP better, you may have some cards that provide protective shielding (Guard Shields). These will directly absorb incoming external damage. The damage absorbed will reduce the power of the Guard Shield until it is empty. In simpler terms, it is your temporary second health bar. Guard Shields are only generated through Card Effects and have an extremely large capacity of absorption. Guard Shields will reset after every combat encounter.
In addition, you may have chosen an ACD that contains a "Healing" card. These will directly regain your own HP, but are very weak in power usually.
